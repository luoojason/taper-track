# Clinical References

The guideline validation logic in taper-track draws on the following published sources. These are provided for transparency and so users can share them with their prescribers.

---

## SSRI / Antidepressant tapering

**Horowitz, M. A., & Taylor, D. (2019).** Tapering of SSRI treatment to mitigate withdrawal symptoms. *The Lancet Psychiatry*, 6(6), 538–546.
[https://doi.org/10.1016/S2215-0366(19)30032-X](https://doi.org/10.1016/S2215-0366(19)30032-X)

Key guidance used: Hyperbolic dose reductions of 5–10% of the current dose every 2–4 weeks to minimise withdrawal symptoms. Reductions exceeding 25% in a two-week period are flagged as potentially rapid.

---

## Benzodiazepine tapering

**Joint Clinical Practice Guideline on Benzodiazepine Tapering: Considerations When Risks Outweigh Benefits (2025).** PMC12463801.
[https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC12463801/](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC12463801/)

Key guidance used: 5–10% dose reductions per 2–4 weeks; slower reductions (2–5%) for long-term users and high-potency benzodiazepines.

**The Ashton Manual** (Professor C. Heather Ashton, 2002, revised 2011). *Benzodiazepines: How They Work and How to Withdraw.*
[https://www.benzo.org.uk/manual/](https://www.benzo.org.uk/manual/)

Reference for benzodiazepine equivalences and cross-tapering guidance. Not a substitute for current clinical guidelines but a widely-cited resource in the patient community.
