# Safety Disclaimer

**taper-track is a tracking tool. It is not medical advice.**

## What this tool does and does not do

This tool helps you follow the taper schedule you and your prescriber agreed on. It tracks your dose schedule, logs your symptoms, and shows you patterns over time.

**It does not:**
- Generate or recommend taper schedules
- Tell you when to change your dose
- Diagnose withdrawal symptoms
- Replace your prescriber's guidance

## Do not change your dose without your prescriber

Changing the timing or size of a dose reduction without your prescriber's guidance can cause serious harm:

- **Benzodiazepines:** Stopping or reducing too quickly can cause seizures, which can be life-threatening. Even a taper that feels "too slow" should be discussed with your prescriber before changing.
- **SSRIs and SNRIs:** Stopping suddenly can cause severe discontinuation syndrome — intense physical and psychological symptoms. Reduction schedules should be gradual and supervised.

## If you are in crisis

If you are having thoughts of harming yourself, please reach out now:

- **US:** Call or text **988** (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline)
- **US:** Text HOME to **741741** (Crisis Text Line)
- **UK:** Call **116 123** (Samaritans, free, 24/7)
- **Canada:** Call or text **988**
- **Australia:** Call **13 11 14** (Lifeline, 24/7)
- **Everywhere else:** [findahelpline.com](https://findahelpline.com)

You can also reach your prescriber or go to your nearest emergency department.

## Accuracy of guideline warnings

The deviation warnings in the schedule entry screen are based on published clinical guidelines (see CLINICAL_REFERENCES.md). They are informational only. Your prescriber's plan is authoritative — the app will let you save any schedule your prescriber gave you, with or without warnings.

## Open source

This app is open source. Any clinician, researcher, or user can read the code. If you find a safety issue, please open an issue on GitHub.
